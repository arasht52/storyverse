## Who He Is

A structural/civil engineer and independent consultant who previously owned and ran a small engineering consultancy that failed, leaving him with debt he has not fully disclosed to anyone. That collapse is why he accepted the Persian Treasure excavation contract. Full canon defined in Character_Bible.md.

---

## Physical Description

- **Appearance:** Lean and wiry rather than muscular, with the kind of build that comes from manual site work rather than exercise. Tired eyes that sharpen noticeably the moment a technical problem is in front of him.
- **Clothing:** Practical, worn-in field clothing — boots that have been resoled at least once, a canvas work jacket with chalk and dust permanently ground into the seams, a battered notebook always in the jacket's inside pocket. Owns exactly one presentable outfit for meetings or town visits and is visibly uncomfortable wearing it.
- **Distinguishing Features:** An old, faint scar across the back of one hand from an early-career site incident, which he will explain factually if asked and never volunteer otherwise. Tends to rub his thumb against his fingers when thinking, a habit crew members have started to read as a sign he's about to ask a hard question.

---

## Daily Routines

- **Morning:** Wakes early regardless of how little he slept, out of long habit rather than energy. Reviews the previous day's site notes before doing much else.
- **Workday:** Structured almost entirely around the excavation's physical and administrative demands — site assessment, documentation, calls with backers. Eats late and often standing up.
- **Evening:** The hardest part of his day to fill. Without site work to structure his attention, he tends to either oversleep from exhaustion or stay up too late re-reading the same field notes, looking for something he might have missed — partly diligence, partly avoidance of sitting with his own thoughts.

---

## Habits and Mannerisms

- Rubs his thumb against his fingers when thinking through a problem — site crew have learned to recognize this as the precursor to a hard question.
- Taps a pen or tool against his palm in a steady, even rhythm when waiting on something he can't control — a self-soothing behavior he is unaware he does.
- Checks his phone for messages from financial backers compulsively, then is visibly relieved or tense depending on what he finds, before resetting his expression within a few seconds.
- Re-reads his own field notes more than is strictly necessary, a habit that has become slightly compulsive since financial stress began compounding.

## Body Language

Holds himself with the careful economy of someone used to fitting into tight or precarious physical spaces — excavation trenches, scaffolding, cramped offices. Stands at a measured, professional distance from people, even those he likes. Crosses his arms not defensively but as a kind of physical bracing when a conversation turns toward something he doesn't want to discuss. His hands are rarely still when he's thinking but go conspicuously still when he's about to say something he's decided in advance to say carefully.

## Voice Rhythm

Even, measured pacing most of the time — the cadence of someone used to explaining technical findings to people who need to trust his judgment. Sentences shorten and flatten under stress rather than speeding up. Rarely raises his volume; when angry, his voice gets quieter and more precisely enunciated, which people who know him find more unsettling than shouting would be.

## Humor Style

Dry, understated, often self-deprecating in a way that's also a deflection — humor as a pressure valve rather than genuine lightness. Tends to make a small joke immediately after admitting something uncomfortable, as if to take the weight off the admission before anyone can respond to it seriously.

## Emotional Triggers

- Being asked directly about the specifics of his finances — triggers immediate deflection.
- Being told he is "imagining things," particularly regarding the impressions — triggers unusually sharp, out-of-character defensiveness.
- Watching someone else take an unnecessary physical risk at the site — triggers an immediate, almost involuntary shift into controlling, directive behavior.
- Mother's avoidance, specifically her redirecting a direct question — triggers visible, controlled frustration he tries and partially fails to hide.

## Likes and Dislikes

- **Likes:** Clear, checkable problems with a definite right answer; quiet, early mornings before anyone else is awake; the specific satisfaction of a structural assessment that confirms his own judgment; simple, functional food and drink rather than anything elaborate; old technical reference material he's owned for years.
- **Dislikes:** Vague, open-ended emotional conversations; being managed or handled by people who think he can't tell when that's happening; waiting on other people's decisions when he can't act himself; being underestimated because of his current financial situation.

## Personal Possessions

- A battered field notebook, carried everywhere; its loss would visibly unsettle him.
- An old technical reference text from his training, kept less for its now-partly-outdated content and more as a tether to a version of himself before the financial collapse.
- A single nicer set of clothing, kept for situations requiring a more presentable appearance, worn with visible discomfort.
- A practical pocketknife or multitool, used constantly at the site and almost never consciously thought about.

## Workspace and Apartment Description

His living space outside the excavation is sparse and underfurnished — not from minimalist preference but because he has been quietly selling or not replacing possessions for some time. On-site, his "workspace" is a folding table and a set of site assessment tools kept in obsessively careful order, in stark visual contrast to the disorder of the rest of his life — the one space he fully controls.

## Sleep Habits

Chronically under-rested, a condition that predates the excavation and worsens as the chapters progress. Falls asleep quickly from exhaustion but wakes frequently, often reaching for his field notebook in the dark before he's even fully conscious.

## Eating Habits

Eats functionally rather than for pleasure under normal circumstances — food as fuel, often eaten standing, often skipped or delayed when work absorbs his attention. Loses appetite further under acute stress, which compounds his physical depletion in ways he does not track or acknowledge as a problem.

## Stress Rituals

Returns to physical, procedural tasks — re-checking measurements, re-organizing his notes, re-walking a section of the site — as a way of regulating stress without having to name what he's stressed about.

## Relationship Behavior

Slow and reluctant to extend trust, but once extended, genuinely loyal — the difficulty is almost entirely in the initial opening, not in sustaining a relationship afterward. Tends to show care through action (covering a cost, taking on a risk himself) rather than through verbal affirmation, which can read as distant to people expecting more direct emotional expression.

## Decision Examples

- **Under financial pressure:** Rather than asking Mother for a loan, he takes a worse-than-ideal travel or logistical arrangement to save money, costing him fatigue rather than dignity.
- **Facing the Industrial Security Officer:** Goes coldly procedural rather than confrontational, citing regulations and assessment protocol rather than engaging emotionally with the officer's authority.
- **After the first impression:** Documents what he experienced in his field notebook in clinical, almost forensic language before allowing himself any emotional reaction to it.

## Dialogue Examples

**With the Local Guide (early, transactional):**
"I need someone who knows this ground better than the survey does. I'm not asking you to vouch for me — I'm asking you to tell me where it's actually safe to dig."

**With the Local Guide (later, after trust has built):**
"You didn't have to come back out here today. I know what people in town are probably saying about you helping me."

**With Mother (deflecting):**
"It's fine. It's a job. I'm not going to pretend it's not strange being out here, but it's just a job."

**With Mother (a rare crack):**
"Why do you keep telling me it's nothing, and then you go quiet every time I bring up where I am?"

**With the Archaeologist (testing, early):**
"I'm not asking you to validate an unauthorized dig. I'm asking you to tell me, professionally, what you think this actually is."

**With the Crow (controlled wariness):**
"I'm listening. That doesn't mean I'm agreeing to anything yet."

**With the Heritage Protection Officer (careful disclosure):**
"I'm not going to pretend this is fully authorized. I am going to tell you exactly what we've found, because I'd rather you hear it from me first."

**With the Rival (clipped, competitive):**
"We're not working together. I don't need to explain why."

## Scene-Specific Behavioral Notes

- In any scene involving the excavation pit directly, his attention to physical hazard should visibly precede any other concern, even financial.
- In any scene involving Mother, his deflection should be shown through subject-changing and procedural language, never through outright hostility — he loves her, even when avoiding her.
- In any scene involving the first or subsequent impressions, his physical exhaustion must be visibly established in the same scene or the immediately preceding one.

## Contradictions

A man who values honesty but is most dishonest with himself about the scale of his own debt and his own growing uncertainty about the impressions. Someone fiercely self-reliant who is, underneath, quietly desperate for someone — anyone — to tell him he is not imagining things, while actively avoiding the conversations that could provide that reassurance.

## Hidden Coping Mechanisms

- Over-documentation as emotional avoidance: the more uncertain or afraid he is, the more obsessively precise his field notes become, as if exactness in one domain could compensate for the lack of it in another.
- Minor, repeated financial self-punishment: choosing the cheaper, harder option for himself specifically even when it isn't strictly necessary, as an unexamined form of penance for the collapse of his firm.
- Selective disclosure as control: choosing exactly how much to tell each person not purely strategically but as a way of feeling like he still controls something in a situation that otherwise feels uncontrollable.

## Small Human Details

- Keeps an old, no-longer-useful piece of identification or paperwork from his training years, well past any practical use for it.
- Hums, almost inaudibly and entirely unconsciously, while doing repetitive technical work — and stops abruptly, looking briefly self-conscious, if he notices someone heard him.
- Carries a small tool given to him early in his career, kept out of habit more than sentiment, though he would be unsettled if he lost it.
- Has a specific, private discomfort with being photographed or documented in ways outside his control, that he has never explained to anyone and would deflect if asked about directly.

## Dialogue Guide

- **Speaking Style:** Precise and economical. Defaults to technical or procedural framing when emotionally cornered. Rarely interrupts, but will cut a conversation short rather than let it drift somewhere he doesn't want to go.
- **Vocabulary:** Engineering and structural terminology used naturally, not performatively; functional Persian-language phrases when speaking with Mother or Father. Avoids dramatic or emotionally loaded language even when the content is emotional.
- **Emotional Range:** Narrow and controlled on the surface — flashes of dry humor, occasional clipped frustration — with rare, hard-won moments of genuine openness that should feel earned, never casual.
- **Things Never Said:** Never states with certainty whether he believes the impressions are real. Never directly asks Mother "what are you hiding," preferring oblique, testing questions. Never voices self-pity, even when the situation would justify it.
- **Recurring Expressions:** A habit of restating a problem in structural terms before responding to it emotionally ("Let's look at what's actually load-bearing here" used both literally and, eventually, as an unconscious metaphor). Tends to end difficult conversations with a short, practical deflection rather than a resolution.

## Production Notes

**Canon vs. placeholder discipline**
- Personality, Narrative Role, Relationships, Gameplay Notes, and Continuity are canon-defined in Character_Bible.md and are not duplicated here. This file is purely a performance and voice reference layered on top of that canon.
- The following biographical facts are NOT established canon and must remain TBD in all scene writing: name, age, exact nationality/citizenship, birthplace, childhood/family-of-origin details, education (institution/location), consultancy's company name/country/city/employers/timeline/dates/clients, precise reason the consultancy failed, and specific financial figures.
- The following biographical facts ARE established canon and may be referenced freely: structural/civil engineer by training and trade, currently an independent consultant, previously owned a small engineering consultancy, that consultancy failed, and the resulting financial collapse led him to accept the Persian Treasure excavation contract.
- Writers must not invent a specific value for any TBD item above without an explicit canon-lock decision. Any scene requiring such detail must use only general, non-specific phrasing (e.g. "the firm," "a client," "a bad year").
- Do not extrapolate a specific nationality, birthplace, or immigration history beyond what Character_Bible.md establishes (Persian-language fluency and a family connection to the Khuzestan region).
- Do not assume a specific named engineering institution, company, or location anywhere in his professional background.

**Continuity flags for writers**
- His financial desperation must remain the visible, primary driver of his choices throughout; any scene that has him act from curiosity or heroism alone, without financial or relational stakes attached, risks drifting from his established motivation.
- His certainty (or lack of it) about the impressions must never resolve before Story_State_Machine.md's PSY-04.
- The elevated fatigue/stress state required to trigger his first impression (EVT-PSY-01) must be read as an acute spike on top of an already-present chronic baseline established in Daily Routines and Sleep Habits above, not as an isolated one-time event.

**Reference priority for writers**
- For canon (motivation, arc, relationships, variables, states, events): consult Character_Bible.md, Story_State_Machine.md, Narrative_Event_Catalog.md, and variables.json.
- For voice, behavior, and performance (how he says things, how he carries himself, how he reacts under pressure): consult this file.
- Use the Dialogue Examples and Internal Monologue Examples above as voice calibration references before drafting any new scene featuring him.
