# Character Bible: Persian Treasure

---

## Character Philosophy

Every character in Persian Treasure exists to apply pressure to Daniel from a distinct, believable direction — financial, legal, historical, familial, or moral. No character exists purely as exposition delivery or as a narrative convenience. Each major character must want something concrete, must be hiding something (whether a secret, a motive, or a vulnerability), and must be changed in some way by the end of the story, even if that change is small or quiet. Characters connected to the ancient-caravan thread must be written with the same ambiguity discipline as the World Bible requires of Daniel's impressions: no character, through dialogue, document, or action, may confirm a supernatural explanation for anything in this story.

---

## Character Design Rules

- Every major character must have a believable, self-interested motivation independent of Daniel's personal arc.
- Every major character must hold back something — information, intent, or feeling — that is revealed only at a narratively appropriate point.
- No character may possess or display confirmed supernatural knowledge, ability, or perception.
- Characters connected to institutions (heritage protection, industrial security, law) must act within the believable limits and incentives of their real-world institution, per World_Bible.md.
- Characters must change measurably over the course of the four chapters — in belief, relationship, or circumstance — never remain static set pieces.
- The historical-layer character(s) must be written so their presence in Daniel's impressions remains consistent with the Rules of Ambiguity in World_Bible.md: every detail Daniel perceives must have a plausible mundane source (research, family story, inference) available alongside the inherited-memory interpretation.
- Detailed biography, dialogue voice, and full relationship tables for each character belong in individual Character_Template.md-based files, not in this document.

## Main Cast Overview

### The Protagonist

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Character ID | character_daniel |
| Narrative Role | Protagonist / sole player-controlled character |
| Story Function | The lens through which every theme — financial pressure, historical ambiguity, heritage risk — is filtered |
| Public Identity | A financially struggling structural/civil engineer hired as a private consultant to stabilize an unauthorized excavation |
| Hidden Truth | The full extent of his debt, and later, the existence of a family connection to this region he was never told about directly |
| Motivation | Resolve crippling personal debt and salvage a stalled professional reputation |
| Internal Conflict | Whether to trust impressions he cannot explain or rationally dismiss, while needing to function as a reliable professional |
| External Conflict | Competing pressure from financial backers, legal/heritage risk, and his own deteriorating physical and financial situation |
| Strengths | Technical competence, structural risk assessment, capacity for careful observation |
| Weaknesses | Financial desperation that clouds judgment, tendency to isolate rather than disclose vulnerability |
| Skills | Structural engineering, site assessment, basic Persian-language fluency |
| Moral Boundaries | Will bend legal and professional rules under pressure, but has limits around endangering others or destroying irreplaceable historical material — exact limits are tested, not fixed in advance |
| Relationship Summary | Central node connected to every other major character; his trust and disclosure choices with each one drive most Relationship-category variables in this package |
| Character Arc | From purely transactional desperation toward a more complicated reckoning with truth, family, and what he is willing to sacrifice to obtain either money or certainty |
| Knowledge Timeline | Begins knowing almost nothing about the historical caravan; accumulates site knowledge, family knowledge, and impression-based knowledge in parallel across the four chapters |
| What the player initially knows | Daniel's financial situation, the basic terms of the contract, and that the excavation is unauthorized |
| What must never be revealed too early | The full nature/source of his family connection to the region, and any information that would tip the ambiguity decisively toward either the supernatural or purely psychological explanation |
